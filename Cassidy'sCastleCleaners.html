Cassidy's Castle Cleaners

CMPM 171/172 Game Development Capstone Project

The collective goal of our 12-person team was to develop a game from start to finish to learn about the various stages of game development. Making this game as our capstone project for the Game Development class at UC Santa Cruz, we had some guidance from our Teaching Assistant who helped us iterate over core mechanics to fit our scope and skillset.

Working in the Unity game engine, we designed an action game themed around cleaning. As a maid, the player has access to multiple skills like a broom sweep or thrown feather dusters to both clean the room and defeat enemies.

Gameplay Video

My Role

As part of the coding team my contributions to this project range from implementing the player's basic interactions, such as movement and basic attack combo, to the core mechanic of cleaning, as well as the camera behavior. Additionally, all of us participated in the design process throughout development as we iterated on ideas during meetings.

Prototype & Iteration

Early attack prototype.

The early stage of this project was crucial in shaping the final game. Early development meant producing quick, tangible prototypes for the team to see the results of the decisions we were making. Doing so helped the Arts team by giving them more time for asset creation, as we used temporary assets as shown in this clip. Since having tight deadlines was a concern for our project, the use of prototypes to quickly convey ideas and playtest material was crucial to iterate efficiently.
